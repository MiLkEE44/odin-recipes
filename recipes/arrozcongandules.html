Arroz con Gandules

Arroz con Gandules, a Puerto Rican classic. This dish contains seasoned rice with pigeon peas and goes great with just about anything.

Ingredients needed

  • 3 cups medium or long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of sofrito
  • 4 ounces of tomato sauce
  • 15 ounces of gandules, paritally drained
  • 2 heaping tablesppons of alcaparrado
  • 1 packet of sazon with achiote
  • 1/2 packet of ham flavoring
  • 1 teaspoon of adobo
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/8 teaspoon oregano
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 cups of hot water

Directions

  1. Rinse rice in water and set aside

  2. In a mdeium caldero or large pot(about 6 quarts or so), heat oil and saute sofrito until softened. Add tomato sauce and simmer for 2 minutes.

  3. Stir in gandules, alcaparrado, all of the spices and 4 cups of water. Taste and adjust the seasonings, adding addional salt 1 teaspoon at a time.
    broth should be heavily seasoned and on the salty side.

  4. Bring ot a rapid boil, then add rice and stir. You may need to add more water to ensure rice is covered by 1 inch of water.
    Reduce heat to a soft boiil and let most of the liquid asbord and evaporate, stir occasionally and gently so the rice does not turn gummy.

  5. Carefully mound rice towards center of pot and cover with lid. Reduce heat to simmer and cook for 20 minutes.
    Stir by folding rice from the bottom up, but do not disturb bottom of pan. Cook for another 20-30 minutes, testing after 20 to see if rice is tender and cooked through.
